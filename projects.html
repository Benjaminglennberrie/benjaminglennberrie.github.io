Pro Bono Net Internship — Expanding access to justice through technology

Supervisor: Simon Rah | Date: Fall 2025

About Pro Bono Net

Pro Bono Net is a nonprofit organization that harnesses technology to expand access to justice for underserved communities. Founded in 1999, it partners with legal aid programs, courts, and community organizations to build digital tools that empower people to understand their rights and help advocates serve them more efficiently.

Its nationally recognized platforms include LawHelp.org, LawHelp Interactive, and Probono.net, all serving low-income families, veterans, immigrants, and disaster survivors.

“Harnessing technology for a more just future.” — Betty Balli Torres

Automation & CMS Migration

My first major project was to automate content migration from Contentful to Payload CMS using n8n. I learned to interpret API documentation, generate and secure API keys, and use HTTP headers for authentication and communication. This project gave me first-hand exposure to backend automation and the fundamentals of API-driven workflows.

AI Chatbot Development & Stress Testing

I edited chatbot functionality within shadow DOM environments and conducted load and stress testing using Python. This work deepened my understanding of asynchronous requests, API rate limits, and debugging in distributed systems. It also strengthened my confidence in combining problem-solving and experimentation to push system reliability boundaries.

Knowledge Management System

I contributed to maintaining and refining the company’s Knowledge Management site built on Google Sites. I ensured updates aligned with organizational standards and documentation consistency. Through this project, I learned how transparency and standardized communication sustain collaboration across large teams.

Directory Initiative

Collaborating with Ken, a PBN team member managing the legal directory, I worked to improve and update listings of legal service organizations nationwide. This project showed the scale of coordination required to maintain accurate legal resources and the real-world consequences of outdated information for vulnerable communities.

Remote Work & Communication

Operating in a fully remote environment taught me professional communication across teams, integrity in self-management, and accountability in distributed workspaces. Early miscommunications became learning experiences that improved my reliability and proactive engagement with supervisors and teammates.

Time Management & Balance

While completing two academic courses and working full-time, I dedicated 20 hours per week to my internship. Balancing these commitments taught me prioritization, endurance, and how to maintain quality under pressure. These habits continue to guide my professional discipline and productivity.

Reflection

My internship at Pro Bono Net was transformative. It broadened my technical capabilities, refined my professionalism, and grounded me in a mission larger than technology itself. The mentors I met and lessons I learned will remain foundational as I advance in my computer science career.
